120 g of an organic compound that contains only carbon and hydrogen gives 330 g of CO2 and 270 g of water on complete combustion. The percentage of carbon and hydrogen, respectively are

A
25 and 75
B
40 and 60
C
60 and 40
D
75 and 25
Correct Option
D

Source
Chemistry • some-basic-concepts-of-chemistry
Explanation

$${C_x}{H_y} + \left( {x + {y \over 4}} \right){O_2} \to xC{O_2} + {y \over 2}{H_2}O$$

From the reaction,

Produced CO2 = x mol

and produced H2O = $${y \over 2}$$ mol

Given produced CO2 = 330 g

$$\therefore$$ moles of CO2 $$ = {{330} \over {44}} = {{30} \over 4} = x$$

Also given produced H2O = 270 gm

$$\therefore$$ Moles of H2O $$ = {{270} \over {18}} = 15 = {y \over 2}$$.

$$\Rightarrow$$ y = 30

$$\therefore$$ $$x:y = {{30} \over 4}:30 = 1:4$$

Formula of the compound = $${(C{H_4})_n}$$

$$\therefore$$ Weight of C in $${(C{H_4})_n}$$ = 12 n

Weight of H in $${(C{H_4})_n}$$ = 4 n

$$\therefore$$ Weight ratio of C and H

= 12 n : 4 n

= 3 : 1

$$\therefore$$ % of C = $${3 \over 4}$$ $$\times$$ 100 = 75

and % of H = $${1 \over 4}$$ $$\times$$ 100 = 25
